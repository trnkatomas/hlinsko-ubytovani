HLINSKO

Hlinsko lies 590 metres high above sea level; the town got its name after remarkable good clay, that was found here in the past, and after bygone craftsmen who worked in this region. We can trace Hlinsko´s history back to the 11th century for the first writen hint at the existence of the place comes from those times. Hlinsko stands on the banks of the Chrudimka river, at the foot of the mountains of the Bohemian - Moravian Uplands. The town is situated on the fringe of the preserve areas of the Žïárské Hills and the Iron Hills. In the middle of the town there is a unique scollection of little cottages called "Bethlehem".

Charming beauty of Hlinsko surroundings has been attracting attention of plenty of artists, especially painters. The artist can find inspiration here and some of them have achieved their best work of art in this area. Grounds of Hlinsko region can offer ideal opportunities of walking, hiking and cycling on well marked out tourist paths and lines. You can go in for all water sports, ball games, tennis, squasche and golf. In winter month the slopes near Hlinsko give tourists opportunity to downhill ski, three ski tows and to sled. Surroundings are ideal for cross - country skiing.
